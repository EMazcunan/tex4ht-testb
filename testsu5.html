3.5   Equation arrays

In the flow of a fluid film we may report

u α = 𝜖 2 κ xxx y − 1 2 y 2 , (1) v = 𝜖 3 κ xxx y , (2) p = 𝜖 κ xx . (3)

Alternatively, the curl of a vector field ( u , v , w ) may be written with only one equation number:

ω 1 = ∂w ∂y − ∂v ∂z , ω 2 = ∂u ∂z − ∂w ∂x , (4) ω 3 = ∂v ∂x − ∂u ∂y .

Whereas a derivation may look like

( p ∧ q ) ∨ ( p ∧ ¬ ⁡ q ) = p ∧ ( q ∨ ¬ ⁡ q ) by distributive law = p ∧ T by excluded middle = p by identity
